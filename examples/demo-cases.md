# Demo Cases

These demo cases are intentionally fictionalized and simplified.
They exist to show how the workflow adapts across different operating contexts.

## Demo case 1 — B2B SaaS founder

### Situation
A founder has product knowledge and strong opinions, but their content sounds too generic.
They post inconsistently and the audience cannot quickly tell why the product is different.

### Workflow
1. define the audience tension
2. map the manual or legacy alternatives
3. write a sharper product promise
4. build 4 content pillars
5. publish a 2-week LinkedIn + X content plan
6. review what language creates qualified conversations

### Expected outputs
- clearer positioning statement
- 3 launch angles
- founder narrative with repeatable hooks
- weekly social cadence

## Demo case 2 — Service business / agency

### Situation
An agency has many capabilities, but the market sees them as “another agency.”
They need a positioning system that turns case-study knowledge into recurring thought leadership.

### Workflow
1. choose the core audience and problem category
2. define the strategic contrast versus generic agency messaging
3. turn process advantages into proof-based content pillars
4. build a content ladder from one client insight into multiple assets
5. run weekly review on response quality, not vanity metrics

### Expected outputs
- sharper category position
- reusable service narrative
- proof-driven content system
- better connection between expertise and lead generation

## Demo case 3 — Ecommerce brand

### Situation
A consumer brand has content volume but weak differentiation.
The team needs a way to connect product benefits, brand point of view, and social storytelling.

### Workflow
1. define the emotional and functional tension in the category
2. position the product against what customers are already using
3. create content pillars across education, proof, lifestyle, and conversion
4. build a 2-week cross-channel calendar
5. review which messages create saves, shares, and product intent

### Expected outputs
- more distinct brand voice
- clearer product framing
- stronger content-to-conversion connection
- reusable campaign structure for future launches

## How to use these cases

Use them as prompts for adaptation, not as scripts to copy word-for-word.
The point is to understand how the operating system changes by audience, product type, and buying context.
