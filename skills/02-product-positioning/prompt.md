# Product Positioning — Claude Prompt

Copy this prompt and paste it into Claude. Fill in the [brackets] with your real information before running.
Requires: completed brand positioning output from skill 01.

---

```
You are a product strategist. I will give you our brand positioning and product details. Help me build a complete product positioning document.

Brand positioning summary:
[paste your brand positioning output here]

Product details:
- Product or feature name: [name]
- User segment for this positioning: [specific user type, not all users]
- Primary use case: [what job are they hiring this product to do?]
- What they use today without us: [specific tool, workflow, or workaround]
- Key proof points: [data, outcomes, process advantages]
- Common objections: [what do people say when they hesitate to buy?]

Using these inputs, produce a complete product positioning document with these sections:

## User and use case
Who specifically, in what specific situation.

## Job to be done
One sentence: what are they trying to accomplish?

## Alternative used today
The real thing they use now — not a fantasy competitor.

## Product promise
Use this exact frame: For [user with situation], [product] helps them [desired result] without [main tradeoff].

## Why this is different
Three to five specific, verifiable differentiators — not feature lists.

## Reasons to believe
Proof for each differentiator above.

## Message hierarchy
- Primary promise
- Supporting claim 1 + proof
- Supporting claim 2 + proof
- Supporting claim 3 + proof

## Objections and answers
For each objection: state it honestly, then answer it directly.

## Launch angles
Three specific angles for content and conversations — each one should be a distinct hook.
```

---

## Tips for better output

- The user segment should be narrower than the brand audience — this is for a specific use case.
- Name the actual alternative. "Other software" is not an answer.
- Each launch angle should be usable as a standalone post topic or sales conversation opener.
- Run the output through `CHECKLIST.md` before passing it to the next skill.
