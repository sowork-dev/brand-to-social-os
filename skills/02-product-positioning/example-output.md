# Example Output — Product Positioning

> This is a fictionalized example continuing the **Clearpath** B2B SaaS scenario from skill 01.
> Clearpath is a delivery exception management platform for logistics operations managers.
> Use it to understand what a strong product positioning output looks like.

---

## User and use case
An operations manager at a 100–300 person third-party logistics (3PL) company who spends 2–3 hours per day manually checking carrier portals and reconciling shipment status across email and spreadsheets.

## Job to be done
Get ahead of delivery exceptions before they create customer complaints, without building a data team or adding headcount.

## Alternative used today
Manual carrier portal checks, exported CSVs loaded into shared Google Sheets, and reactive Slack threads when customers report problems.

## Product promise
For operations managers at mid-size 3PLs, Clearpath helps them catch and resolve delivery exceptions before customers notice, without requiring a data analyst or custom integration project.

## Why this is different
- Proactive exception detection, not reactive shipment tracking
- Connected to 200+ carrier APIs out of the box, no IT project required
- Exceptions surfaced 6–12 hours before the standard carrier update cycle
- Built for operations managers to use without data or engineering support
- Surfaces the reason for the exception, not just the status change

## Reasons to believe
- 200+ pre-built carrier API connections, live on day one
- Median customer setup time: under one business day
- Exception detection happens before customers escalate in 78% of monitored shipments
- Built by a founding team that previously ran 3PL operations for 8 years

## Message hierarchy

**Primary promise:** Know about delivery problems before your customers do.

**Supporting claim 1:** Connect all your carriers in a day, not a quarter.
Proof: 200+ pre-built integrations, no IT ticket required.

**Supporting claim 2:** Spot exceptions 6–12 hours earlier than manual checks.
Proof: Clearpath monitors carrier data continuously, not just during business hours.

**Supporting claim 3:** Built for ops managers, not data teams.
Proof: Customers self-onboard with zero engineering involvement.

## Objections and answers

**"We already use a TMS."**
Most TMS tools show shipment status. Clearpath shows exception risk before the status changes. They solve different problems and most customers run both.

**"Our team handles this in spreadsheets and it works fine."**
It works until a customer finds out before you do. That is the moment that damages trust. Clearpath is designed specifically to close that gap.

**"We do not have budget for another tool."**
One escalated complaint that churns a client costs more than a year of Clearpath. We can model the math with you.

## Launch angles

1. **The reactive ops story:** The pain of being the last person in the room to know about a delivery failure — and what proactive visibility actually looks like.
2. **The spreadsheet trap:** Why logistics teams keep building more complex spreadsheets instead of solving the underlying data problem, and the cost of that pattern.
3. **The carrier portal myth:** Why checking portals manually creates a false sense of control and what the data actually shows about the timing gap.
