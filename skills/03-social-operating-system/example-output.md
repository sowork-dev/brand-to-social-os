# Example Output - Social Operating System

> Clearpath scenario. Solo founder. LinkedIn primary. 3 posts per week.

## Goals

1. Build a point of view on proactive ops within 8 weeks
2. Generate 5 qualified conversations per month by week 6
3. Identify best-performing message angle by week 8

## Channel roles

LinkedIn: Operations managers use it for professional learning and vendor research. Credibility trust. Long-form and frameworks work here.

## Content pillars

Pillar 1 - The visibility problem: Educate on why reactive tracking causes ops firefighting. Data posts, before/after comparisons.

Pillar 2 - Operations mental models: Share how good ops teams think. Frameworks, opinion posts, process walkthroughs.

Pillar 3 - The integration myth: Challenge the belief that better tracking needs a big IT project. Myth-busting posts, objection responses.

Pillar 4 - Behind the build: Humanize the company. Founder story, product decision reasoning.

Pillar 5 - Proof and outcomes: Convert curiosity to action. Case studies (anonymized), metric-led posts.

## Weekly cadence

Monday: Pillar 1 or 2, educational or opinion, 150-300 words
Wednesday: Pillar 3 or 4, challenge or behind-the-build, 80-150 words
Friday: Pillar 5 or experiment post

## Engagement rules

Reply within 4 hours on weekdays for any comment with a question or disagreement.
Meaningful conversation: anyone who shares a specific problem from their own experience.
Unexpected traction: note the hook and repeat the angle next week with a different proof point.

## Repurposing flow

One founder observation becomes: Monday LinkedIn post, Wednesday shorter take, one X thread, one email block, one DM conversation starter.

## Experiment backlog

1. Contrarian title vs how-to framing on pillar 2 (weeks 3-4)
2. Customer story vs statistic post on pillar 5 (weeks 4-5)
3. 7am vs 12pm posting time (weeks 5-6)
4. 80-word vs 250-word on pillar 1 (weeks 6-7)
5. Direct demo CTA post effect on overall engagement (week 8)

## Weekly review questions

1. Which post got the best comment quality, not just the most likes?
2. Did anyone describe a problem we have not addressed yet?
3. Which pillar felt easiest to write? Which felt forced?
4. Did any post create a conversation that moved to DMs or a meeting?
5. What is one thing to do differently next week?
