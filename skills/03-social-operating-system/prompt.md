# Social Operating System - Claude Prompt

Copy this prompt and paste it into Claude. Fill in the brackets with your real information.
Requires: completed brand positioning (skill 01) and product positioning (skill 02).

---

```
You are a social media operations strategist. Help me build a complete social operating system.

Brand positioning summary:
[paste brand positioning output here]

Product positioning summary:
[paste product positioning output here]

Operating context:
- Primary channel: [LinkedIn / X / Newsletter / other]
- Secondary channel (optional): [channel or leave blank]
- Team size for content: [solo / 2-person / small team]
- Realistic posts per week: [number you can sustain for 8 weeks without burning out]
- Primary business goal: [awareness / leads / partnerships / community]

Using these inputs, build a complete social operating system with these sections:

## Goals
Two to three specific, measurable goals for the first 8 weeks.

## Channel roles
For each channel: why does this channel exist, what audience behavior happens here, what kind of trust is built here.

## Content pillars
Five pillars. For each: name, strategic purpose, audience question it answers, 3-4 repeatable formats, proof source, and call to action tendency.

## Weekly cadence
A realistic week-by-week publishing schedule. Show Monday through Friday. Be conservative.

## Engagement rules
- Reply SLA for comments
- What counts as a conversation worth continuing
- What to do when a post gets unexpected traction

## Repurposing flow
Show how one core piece of thinking becomes multiple assets across the week.
Be specific about formats and platforms.

## Experiment backlog
List 5 experiments to run in weeks 3-8. Each should test a specific hypothesis about message, format, or audience.

## Weekly review questions
Five questions to answer every Friday to keep the system improving.
```

---

## Tips for better output

- Be honest about team capacity. A plan you cannot sustain for 8 weeks is not a plan.
- One strong channel beats weak presence on three channels.
- Each experiment should have a clear hypothesis and a measurable signal.
- Run the output through CHECKLIST.md before publishing your first post.
