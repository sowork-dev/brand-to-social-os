# Example Output - Content Pillar Design

> Clearpath scenario. LinkedIn. Operations managers.

## Pillar 1: The visibility problem (Education)
Purpose: Teach the market that reactive tracking is the root cause of ops firefighting.
Audience question: Why does my team always find out about problems too late?
Formats: stat + context, before/after, 5-step framework, common mistake post
Proof source: logistics industry data, internal benchmarks, customer intake conversations
CTA tendency: saves, shares, comments with own experience

## Pillar 2: Ops mental models (Point of view)
Purpose: Build credibility by showing how proactive ops teams think differently.
Audience question: What separates reactive ops from proactive ops?
Formats: numbered mental model, contrarian opinion, process walkthrough, unpopular truth
Proof source: 8 years 3PL experience, customer pattern recognition
CTA tendency: discussion, follow, DM requesting the framework

## Pillar 3: Real evidence (Proof)
Purpose: Convert interest into action with specific verifiable outcomes.
Audience question: Does this actually work for companies like mine?
Formats: anonymized case study, metric snapshot, before/after workflow, quote + context
Proof source: customer results, onboarding benchmarks, setup time records
CTA tendency: demo request, share with a colleague

## Pillar 4: Behind the build (Behind the scenes)
Purpose: Humanize the company and demonstrate decision quality.
Audience question: Who built this and do they understand logistics?
Formats: founder decision story, product reasoning, what we got wrong, team perspective
Proof source: founding team background, product history, retrospectives
CTA tendency: follow, connection request, referral

## Pillar 5: Next step invitation (Conversion)
Purpose: Invite next step without making the feed promotional.
Audience question: How do I know if this is right for my operation?
Formats: use case fit post, objection + CTA, results + invitation, honest limitation post
Proof source: customer fit patterns, ICP situations, onboarding data
CTA tendency: demo request, DM to discuss fit
