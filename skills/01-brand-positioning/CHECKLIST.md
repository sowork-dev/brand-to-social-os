# Brand Positioning — Pre-flight Checklist

Run this before starting the brand positioning workflow.
A weak input produces a weak output. This checklist catches gaps early.

## Inputs ready?

- [ ] I can describe the primary audience in one specific sentence (not "SMBs" or "everyone")
- [ ] I know what they are doing today instead of using my product
- [ ] I can name at least one real incumbent, manual process, or workaround they rely on
- [ ] I have at least one proof point (outcome, credential, process advantage, or lived experience)
- [ ] I have a point of view on what is broken in the category

## Positioning clarity check

- [ ] My positioning statement names a specific audience — not a vague segment
- [ ] My strategic contrast names a real alternative — not a fantasy competitor
- [ ] My differentiation is observable — a customer could verify it, not just feel it
- [ ] My tone and voice feel distinct from generic category language
- [ ] A content team could create 30 posts from this output without sounding generic

## Red flags — stop and fix before continuing

- [ ] The audience definition could apply to any company in the category → too broad
- [ ] The differentiation is a feature list, not a strategic contrast → reframe
- [ ] The positioning statement sounds like a tagline, not a strategic claim → go deeper
- [ ] The proof stack is empty or hypothetical → find real evidence first
