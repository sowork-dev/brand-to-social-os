# Brand Positioning

Use this skill when you need to define what a company stands for before writing product copy or running content.

## Inputs
- business model
- target audience
- existing alternatives
- category context
- proof points
- founder or company point of view

## Workflow

### 1. Define the audience tension
Answer:
- what situation are they stuck in?
- what are they tolerating today?
- what feels expensive, slow, fragmented, or embarrassing?

### 2. Map the alternative
Document what the audience does instead:
- incumbent vendor
- manual workflow
- spreadsheet
- freelancer
- agency
- “do nothing”

### 3. Identify the strategic contrast
Complete this sentence:

> Most of this market still does ___.
> We believe the better model is ___.

### 4. Build the proof stack
List proof in order:
- lived experience
- proprietary insight
- process advantage
- operating speed
- customer outcome

### 5. Write the positioning core
Produce:
- one-line positioning statement
- audience definition
- category frame
- tension statement
- differentiation bullets
- tone and point of view

## Output format

```markdown
# Brand Positioning

## Audience
## Core tension
## What they use today
## Why that breaks
## Our strategic contrast
## Positioning statement
## Reasons to believe
## Voice principles
```

## Quality bar

A strong output should be sharp enough that a content team can create 30 posts without sounding generic.
