# Example Output — Brand Positioning

> This is a fictionalized example for a B2B SaaS company called **Clearpath** that helps operations managers at mid-size logistics companies reduce manual tracking work.
> Use it to understand what a strong output looks like before running your own.

---

## Audience
Operations managers at 50–500 person logistics and fulfillment companies who are still running shipment tracking and exception management in spreadsheets and email threads.

## Core tension
They are responsible for delivery performance but have no real-time visibility. They find out about problems from customers, not their own systems.

## What they use today
A combination of carrier portals, manual CSV exports, shared spreadsheets, and Slack threads. Some have legacy TMS tools that do not surface exceptions clearly.

## Why that breaks
Each of those systems shows a slice of data but none of them connects the dots. The manager becomes the integration layer. When something goes wrong, they are always the last to know.

## Our strategic contrast
Most tracking tools show you where your shipment is.
We show you what is about to go wrong and why, before your customer calls.

## Positioning statement
Clearpath helps operations managers at mid-size logistics companies catch delivery exceptions before they become customer complaints, without building a custom data team.

## Reasons to believe
- Built by former operations managers who ran 3PLs, not by software generalists
- Connects to 200+ carrier APIs without custom integration work
- Surfaces exceptions 6–12 hours earlier than manual tracking workflows
- Customers reduce inbound complaint volume by 40% in the first 60 days
- No data team required — setup takes less than one business day

## Voice principles
- Speak like an operator, not a software vendor
- Name the real problem before offering the solution
- Use specific numbers when available; avoid vague superlatives
- Respect the reader's experience — they have seen bad software before
- Short sentences. Direct claims. No filler phrases.
