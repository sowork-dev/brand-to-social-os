# Quality Gates -- Master Reference

This document consolidates all quality gates, scoring rubrics, and minimum thresholds
for all 6 skills in the Brand to Social OS.

Use this file to:
- Quickly verify whether a skill output is ready to deliver
- Calibrate AI agent output quality before committing to context files
- Train new team members on what 'good' looks like for each skill

---

## How quality gates work in this OS

Every skill has two types of quality controls:

**Binary gates:** Pass/fail checks. If any gate fails, the output is not deliverable.
The agent must collect more information and revise before delivering.

**Scoring rubric:** 1-5 scale across 6 dimensions. Each skill has a minimum score
to deliver. If the score falls below the minimum, the agent must ask for clarification
and revise specific sections.

Rule: quality gates and scoring happen BEFORE the final output is delivered to the user.
The agent runs the gates internally, states the score in the output, and flags
any sections that were borderline.

## Skill 01 -- Brand Positioning

**Minimum delivery score: 25 / 30**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Audience | Names a specific person in a specific situation | 'Small businesses' or 'marketers' |
| Alternatives | Names a real tool or behaviour | 'Other solutions' or 'traditional methods' |
| Strategic contrast | Specific enough that a competitor would disagree | Applies to any company |
| Positioning statement | One sentence, fills the template exactly | Multiple sentences or vague |
| Proof points | Specific and verifiable | 'We have years of experience' |
| Voice principles | Includes do / do-not examples | Abstract tone descriptions |

### Scoring rubric

| Section | 5 -- Excellent | 3 -- Acceptable | 1 -- Fail |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Audience | Specific person + situation + trigger | Named role, no situation | 'Everyone' |
| Core tension | Specific cost with mechanism | Names pain, not cause | Vague frustration |
| Strategic contrast | POV a competitor would dispute | Generic observation | Not present |
| Proof stack | 4-6 verifiable specific points | 2-3 somewhat specific | Generic claims |
| Voice principles | 3-5 rules with do/do-not examples | Rules without examples | 'Professional and friendly' |
| Output clarity | Team could write 30 posts from this | Needs 1-2 clarifications | Needs full session |

## Skill 02 -- Product Positioning

**Minimum delivery score: 24 / 30**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Job to be done | Starts with a verb, names outcome, names tradeoff | 'Users want to save time' |
| Alternative | Names specific tool or behaviour | 'Competitors' or 'manual processes' |
| Product promise | Fills template exactly, one sentence | Multiple sentences or feature description |
| Differentiation | 3 mechanism-level differences | 3 marketing claims |
| Proof points | Numbers or named outcomes | 'Great results' |
| Objection handling | Names root cause, not just surface | Lists objections without addressing |

### Scoring rubric

| Section | 5 -- Excellent | 3 -- Acceptable | 1 -- Fail |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| User + use case | Specific person + specific situation | Named role, vague situation | 'Businesses' |
| Job to be done | Verb + outcome + tradeoff | Has verb, vague outcome | Feature description |
| Product promise | Fills template exactly | Close but incomplete | Not present |
| Differentiation | 3 mechanism differences vs named alternative | 2 mechanisms + 1 claim | 3 claims |
| Proof points | 4+ with numbers or named outcomes | 2-3 somewhat specific | Generic |
| Launch angles | 3 distinct, each usable as post/pitch | 2 angles, 1 vague | 1 or none |

## Skill 03 -- Social Operating System

**Minimum delivery score: 22 / 30**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Cadence | Set to what team can sustain for 8 weeks | Set to aspiration, not reality |
| Channel roles | Each channel has defined role and success signal | 'We post everywhere' |
| Repurposing ratio | 1 idea to 3+ pieces planned | 1 idea to 1 post |
| Engagement rules | Specific SLAs and quality standards | 'We engage with our community' |
| Experiment backlog | 5+ active experiments queued | No experiments planned |

### Scoring rubric

| Section | 5 -- Excellent | 3 -- Acceptable | 1 -- Fail |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Channel roles | Role, format, cadence, signal per channel | 2-3 channels defined | All channels together |
| Weekly loop | Specific days, owners, time estimates | General weekly plan | Post regularly |
| Topic backlog | Source list + scoring system | Topic list without scoring | No system |
| Repurposing | Specific formats named with ratio target | General repurposing mentioned | Not present |
| Engagement rules | SLAs + quality standards | Reply timing only | Not present |
| Experiment system | Active backlog with hypotheses | Experiments mentioned | No plan |

## Skill 04 -- Content Pillar Design

**Minimum delivery score: 22 / 30**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Strategic purpose | Each pillar has distinct business reason | Five variations of same content type |
| Audience question | Specific question audience is asking | 'What do we want them to know' |
| Brand POV expressed | Each pillar connects to strategic contrast | Generic topic area |
| Proof source | Named and accessible | 'The internet' |
| Format variety | At least 3 repeatable formats per pillar | 1 format per pillar |

### Scoring rubric

| Section | 5 -- Excellent | 3 -- Acceptable | 1 -- Fail |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Pillar distinctness | Each serves different strategic purpose | 3-4 distinct, 1-2 overlap | Multiple indistinguishable |
| Audience question | Specific question they actually ask | Reasonable but vague | What we want them to know |
| POV alignment | Each clearly expresses strategic contrast | Most aligned | Disconnected from positioning |
| Proof source | Named sources or data categories | General category | 'The internet' |
| Format variety | 3+ repeatable formats per pillar | 2 formats each | 1 or none |
| CTA logic | Matches pillar purpose | Reasonable CTA noted | No CTA logic |

## Skill 05 -- Performance Review

**Minimum delivery score: 20 / 30**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Winner analysis | Names specific mechanism (hook + topic + proof) | 'It did well' |
| Loser diagnosis | Names what broke (hook / topic / format / timing) | 'It did not get traction' |
| Audience quality | Categorised by buyer fit | Impressions and likes only |
| Business relevance | Counts real business actions | Engagement rate as primary metric |
| Next experiments | Specific hypothesis + variable + success criteria | 'We will try different content' |

### Scoring rubric

| Section | 5 -- Excellent | 3 -- Acceptable | 1 -- Fail |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Winners analysis | Mechanism identified, repeat version planned | Top performers noted | Most liked listed |
| Losers diagnosis | Root cause identified | Acknowledged as weak | Not discussed |
| Audience quality | Categorised with buyer fit assessment | General impression | Not evaluated |
| Business relevance | Real actions counted and categorised | Mentioned | Not tracked |
| Experiment decisions | Hypothesis + variable + success criteria | Experiments mentioned | None planned |
| Next week plan | 2-3 specific repeats + 1-3 experiments | General direction | No decisions |

## Skill 06 -- LinkedIn Operator Pack

**Minimum hook score: 8 / 10**
**Minimum post score: 32 / 40**

### Binary gates (all must pass)

| Gate | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Hook | Creates tension or curiosity, no banned openers | Starts with I, We, Excited or no tension |
| Paragraphs | Maximum 3 lines each | Wall of text |
| Specificity | Names numbers, situations, or real examples | General principles only |
| CTA | One action, matches pillar purpose | Multiple CTAs or no CTA |
| Voice | Matches voice principles from positioning | Generic marketing tone |

### Hook scoring rubric (10 points)

| Criterion | 10 | 7-9 | 5-6 | 1-4 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Stops the scroll | Irresistible | Interesting | Generic | Skippable |
| Specificity | Precise number/name/situation | Some specificity | Vague | Abstract |
| Tension | Strong open loop | Some tension | Mild interest | None |
| Voice alignment | Clearly this brand | Mostly aligned | Neutral | Off brand |
| No banned openers | Zero banned words | -- | -- | Contains banned opener |

### Post scoring rubric (40 points)

| Criterion | 10 | 7 | 5 | 1 |
|-----------|-----|---|---|---|
| Hook | Irresistible, passes scoring | Strong, slightly generic | Acceptable | Banned opener |
| Substance | Specific claim + evidence + insight | Good, light on evidence | Correct but generic | Opinion, no support |
| Voice | Reads exactly like the brand | Mostly on brand | Neutral | Off brand |
| CTA logic | Single action, matches pillar | Present, slightly misaligned | Weak | None or multiple |

## Common quality failure patterns

These are the most common ways outputs fail the quality gates across all 6 skills:

**Pattern 1 -- Audience stays vague throughout all skills**
If skill 01 produces a vague audience definition, every downstream skill will inherit
the vagueness. The problem compounds. The earlier the vague audience is caught and
corrected, the less rework required downstream. Quality gate on skill 01 audience
is the most important gate in the entire OS.

**Pattern 2 -- Proof inventory is empty**
Skills 02 (proof points), 04 (proof pillar), and 06 (proof posts) all require real proof.
If the brand has no verifiable outcomes yet, these sections will either be empty or
filled with invented examples. Invented proof is worse than no proof -- it is
detectable by buyers and damages trust. Flag missing proof early in skill 01.

**Pattern 3 -- Strategic contrast is too safe**
The most common reason a positioning output scores 18-22/30 instead of 25+/30 is a
strategic contrast that is technically true but not specific enough to differentiate.
Any contrast that a competitor could post on their website without embarrassment is
not sharp enough. Test every contrast by asking: would our closest competitor agree
with this statement? If yes, rewrite it.

**Pattern 4 -- Experiments are planned but never run**
The experiment system in skill 03 and the experiment review in skill 05 only work if
experiments are actually executed. The most reliable signal that the operating system
has broken down is when skill 05 consistently shows zero completed experiments.

**Pattern 5 -- Output scores are not included in deliverables**
Every skill output should include a score at the bottom. If the score is missing,
the quality gate was not run. Quality scores are not optional -- they are the
feedback loop that improves output quality over time.
