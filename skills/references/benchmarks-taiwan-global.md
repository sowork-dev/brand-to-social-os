## LinkedIn Benchmarks — Global (2025-2026)

### Engagement rate by follower count

| Follower count | Below average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Under 1,000 | < 2% | 2-4% | 4-8% | > 8% |
| 1,000 - 5,000 | < 1.5% | 1.5-3% | 3-6% | > 6% |
| 5,000 - 20,000 | < 1% | 1-2% | 2-5% | > 5% |
| 20,000 - 100,000 | < 0.5% | 0.5-1.5% | 1.5-3% | > 3% |
| 100,000+ | < 0.3% | 0.3-0.8% | 0.8-2% | > 2% |

### Impressions per post by follower count

| Follower count | Below average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Under 1,000 | < 200 | 200-600 | 600-2,000 | > 2,000 |
| 1,000 - 5,000 | < 500 | 500-2,000 | 2,000-6,000 | > 6,000 |
| 5,000 - 20,000 | < 2,000 | 2,000-8,000 | 8,000-20,000 | > 20,000 |
| 20,000 - 100,000 | < 8,000 | 8,000-30,000 | 30,000-80,000 | > 80,000 |

### Comments per post

| Level | Count | What it signals |
|---|---|---|
| No signal | 0-2 | Post reached the wrong audience or hook did not land |
| Early traction | 3-10 | Topic resonates, experiment worth repeating |
| Strong signal | 10-25 | Core message landed, worth writing a follow-up series |
| Viral signal | 25+ | Repost as thread, newsletter, and DM campaign |

### Post format benchmarks (LinkedIn, 2025)

| Format | Avg reach multiplier vs text-only | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Text only | 1.0x (baseline) | POV, behind the scenes, short insights |
| Image + text | 0.8x | Brand visuals, infographics — lower reach than text |
| Native document / carousel | 1.5-2.5x | Step-by-step frameworks, proof, education |
| Poll | 1.8x | Testing message angles, community engagement |
| Video (native upload) | 1.2-1.8x | Founder story, demonstration, proof |
| Article (long-form) | 0.4x | Archive and SEO only — not for reach |

> Note: Image posts get lower reach than pure text in 2025. Use carousels or text-only for reach.
> Never put external links in post body — LinkedIn suppresses posts with outbound links.

### Publishing cadence — what the data shows

| Cadence | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 1 post / week | Presence maintained, slow growth, algorithm treats account as low-priority |
| 2-3 posts / week | Steady growth, minimum viable for most small teams |
| 4-5 posts / week | Accelerated growth, requires strong topic backlog |
| 1+ posts / day | Fast growth if quality holds, risk of burnout and quality drop |

Consistency beats frequency. 3 posts/week for 6 months > 5 posts/week for 6 weeks then silence.

## Taiwan Brand Going Global — Specific Patterns (2024-2026)

Based on observation of Taiwan B2B and media accounts publishing in English on LinkedIn.

### Common engagement patterns

| Situation | Typical result | What to do instead |
|---|---|---|
| Taiwan brand posts in Chinese on LinkedIn | 70-80% of reach is Taiwan-based audience, not target market | Publish primary content in English, use Chinese for Taiwan-specific announcements |
| Brand posts product features without POV | Engagement rate < 0.5%, zero DMs | Lead with the problem or tension, feature is the proof not the hook |
| Brand posts translated press releases | Near-zero engagement, sometimes negative reaction | Cut to the insight, remove corporate framing |
| Brand posts about awards or milestones | High likes from colleagues, zero from buyers | Use the award as proof inside a problem-focused post, not as the main topic |
| Brand posts about company culture | Engaged by job seekers, not buyers | Keep to < 10% of content mix unless talent acquisition is the goal |

### Audience building timeline for Taiwan brands (realistic)

| Timeline | What to expect |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1-4 | Mostly Taiwanese followers, low engagement from target market |
| Months 2-3 | First non-Taiwan followers if POV is clear and English is strong |
| Months 3-6 | Engagement rate peaks as algorithm learns audience; first inbound DMs from qualified buyers |
| Months 6-12 | Compounding reach if consistency holds; content quality becomes the bottleneck |

### Language decisions

| Goal | Language recommendation |
|---|---|
| Build audience in US/EU | English-only LinkedIn |
| Build audience in Japan | Japanese-first, English subtitle acceptable |
| Build audience in SEA | English-first; Bahasa or Thai for specific market posts |
| Build audience in Greater China | LinkedIn weak — use WeChat, Xiaohongshu instead |
| Taiwan domestic audience | Chinese on Facebook/Instagram; LinkedIn reach is low |

## Brand Positioning — Quality Benchmarks

These are signals of a strong vs weak brand positioning output.

| Dimension | Weak | Strong |
|---|---|---|
| Audience specificity | Marketing managers at B2B companies | Head of marketing at Series A SaaS companies in SEA preparing first US expansion |
| Strategic contrast | We are different because we care more | Most agencies sell deliverables. We sell positioning decisions. |
| Proof point | 10 years of experience | 47 brand positioning projects across hardware, SaaS, and media; 3 resulted in category creation |
| Voice principle | Professional and approachable | Never use passive voice. Say what you mean. Example: use cuts, not reduces friction. |

## LinkedIn Post — Quality Benchmarks

### Hook quality examples

| Quality | Hook example | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Poor | We are excited to share our new report on Taiwan brands going global | Banned opener, no tension, sounds like a press release |
| Below average | Here are 5 tips for Taiwan brands expanding overseas | Generic, no specific tension, any brand could write this |
| Average | Most Taiwan brands fail in the US market for the same reason | Better tension but no specificity yet |
| Good | Taiwan brands spend 18 months on product localisation. Then they write US copy that sounds like a translation. | Specific, creates tension, names the exact mistake |
| Excellent | I reviewed 40 Taiwan brand LinkedIn accounts. 37 had the same problem on line 1. | Specific number, stakes are high, creates strong curiosity |

### Post length benchmarks

| Length | Words | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Short-form | 50-80 words | Quick insight, experiment format, reaction post |
| Standard | 100-150 words | Most POV and behind-the-scenes posts |
| Long-form | 150-300 words | Education, proof, detailed case study |
| Over-length | 300+ words | Almost never on LinkedIn — break into series instead |

## Performance Review — Signal Thresholds

Use these thresholds when interpreting weekly review data.

### When to repeat a post approach
- Engagement rate > 3% (for accounts under 5,000 followers)
- 3+ substantive comments (not emoji reactions)
- Any inbound DM from a buyer-profile connection
- Saves > 2% of impressions (signals educational value)

### When to retire a post approach
- Engagement rate < 0.5% across 3+ attempts with the same format
- Zero comments after 48 hours on 2+ posts of the same type
- Engagement only from peer accounts, zero from buyer profile

### Experiment minimum viable data

| What you are testing | Minimum posts before concluding | Minimum impressions |
|---|---|---|
| Hook format | 3 posts per format | 1,000 per post |
| Post length | 4 posts per length variant | 1,000 per post |
| Content pillar mix | 2 full weeks of data | N/A |
| CTA type | 3 posts per CTA variant | 1,000 per post |
