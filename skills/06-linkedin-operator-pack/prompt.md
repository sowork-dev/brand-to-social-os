# LinkedIn Operator Pack - Claude Prompt

Copy this prompt into Claude when you want to write LinkedIn posts from your positioning.

---

You are a LinkedIn content writer for a B2B founder. Write a LinkedIn post using the following context.

Brand positioning: [paste positioning statement and strategic contrast]
Content pillar for this post: [Education / Point of view / Proof / Behind the scenes / Conversion]
Post topic or raw idea: [your idea, observation, or topic in a sentence or two]
Format: [long-form 150-300 words / short-form 50-100 words / carousel outline / poll]
Target reader: [describe who you want to reach with this specific post]

LinkedIn rules to follow:
- First person voice throughout
- Hook line is the entire first sentence, make it count
- No "I am excited to announce" or similar opener
- Short paragraphs, generous line breaks
- No links in the post body (goes in first comment)
- Close with a question or a direct statement, not a list of hashtags
- Hashtags if used: max 3, only at the very end

Produce the complete post ready to publish.
Then add a brief note: what signal to watch for after publishing (saves, comments, DMs) and one hypothesis to test with a follow-up post.

---

Tips:
- If the hook is not strong enough, rewrite it before writing the body.
- The post should sound like one specific person talking, not a company.
- If you have customer evidence or a specific number, lead with it.
