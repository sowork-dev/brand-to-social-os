# Example Output - LinkedIn Operator Pack

> Three posts for Clearpath. Operations managers. Logistics.

## Post 1: Education

Hook: Your ops team is always the last to know about delivery failures. That is not a staffing problem. It is a data visibility problem.

Body:
Most logistics teams check carrier portals manually. Once in the morning, maybe once after lunch.

But carriers update exception data continuously.

By the time your team sees a problem, the customer has often already noticed.

The gap is not effort. The gap is timing.

Close: How much time passes in your operation between when a shipment goes wrong and when your team finds out?

Format: long-form
Pillar: Education
Notes: Watch for comments with specific timing numbers. These are gold for follow-up posts.

## Post 2: Point of view

Hook: Most tracking tools are built to answer "where is my shipment." The better question is "what is about to go wrong."

Body:
Status visibility and exception intelligence are different products.

One tells you what happened.
The other tells you what to prevent.

I spent 8 years running 3PL operations before building Clearpath. The carriers had real-time data. We were the bottleneck.

That is still how most operations run today.

Close: The data already exists. The question is whether your system surfaces it before your customer does.

Format: long-form
Pillar: Point of view
Notes: Testing "two different products" framing. Watch whether readers engage with the contrast.

## Post 3: Proof

Hook: One of our customers reduced inbound complaint volume by 40% in their first 60 days. They did not hire anyone new.

Body:
They were a 3PL handling about 2,000 shipments per week.

Before: exceptions discovered when customers called. 2 hours per day on manual portal checks.

After: exceptions surface automatically, 6-12 hours before the standard carrier update cycle. They now contact customers about problems before customers contact them.

Close: If you want to see the workflow they use, reply or send me a message.

Format: short-form with metric
Pillar: Proof
